What is Mutual Aid?

Mutual aid is about community members supporting each other based on need, not transactional exchange. We help each other because we're all interconnected, and everyone has something to offer.

Principles:

  • Solidarity, not charity
  • Give what you can, take what you need
  • No one is free until we're all free
  • Build community power together

üÜò How to Request Help

When you need support:

  1. Don't hesitate to ask! We all need help sometimes.
  2. Post in [communication channel - Matrix, bulletin board, etc.]
  3. OR contact [mutual aid coordinator name and contact]
  4. Be specific about what you need
  5. Include timing (urgent vs. flexible)
Examples of requests:
  • "Need help moving furniture on Saturday morning"
  • "Could use a ride to doctor's appointment next Tuesday"
  • "Looking for childcare swap - I watch your kids Monday, you watch mine Wednesday"
  • "Extra vegetables from my garden - come take what you need!"

üôã How to Offer Help

Ways to contribute:

  • Respond to specific requests
  • Offer ongoing support (weekly help with something)
  • Share surplus resources or food
  • Teach a skill
  • Provide emotional support or companionship
  • Contribute to community fund

It's okay if you can't always help! Your capacity varies, and that's normal.

üìú Mutual Aid Agreements

Our commitments:

  • We practice abundance mindset - there's enough for everyone
  • We give freely without expectation of return
  • We receive gratefully without shame
  • We honor people's capacity and boundaries
  • We communicate clearly about needs and offers
  • We center those most impacted by systems of oppression

Boundaries are healthy!

  • It's okay to say no
  • It's okay to ask for help
  • It's okay to change your mind
  • It's okay to have limited capacity

üîß When Things Go Wrong

Sometimes mutual aid situations become complicated or conflict arises. That's part of being in community.

If you're feeling resentful, overwhelmed, or hurt:

  1. Take a breath - your feelings are valid
  2. Try direct communication if you feel safe
  3. Reach out to [conflict support person/team]
  4. We have a conflict resolution process: [Describe or link to it]

Repair is possible! Most conflicts can be worked through with care.
